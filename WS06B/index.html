Neuroscience in a nutshell

Neuroscience delves into the physiological structure and function of the brain and nervous system. With around a hundred billion neurons, the challenge is to map the intricate networks that process thoughts and behaviors, revealing the brain's adaptability through neuroplasticity. This field combines biology and chemistry to explore issues like pain perception and diseases such as Parkinson's, utilizing tools like computer simulations and brain imaging to learn about the brain's complex anatomy and its connections to the mind and body.

Cognitive Science in a nutshell

Cognitive Science is an interdisciplinary field focused on understanding the information processes of the mind, investigating aspects such as perception, action, language, and memory. Cognitive Science also examines mental representations, compares human and artificial intelligence, and explores the biological foundations of cognition. This field applies disciplines like philosophy, neuroscience, and anthropology to comprehend how the mind and brain work together, aiming to improve areas like education and technology through insights into intelligence and behavior.

Questions Neuroscience asks

  • What causes neurological disorders like Alzheimer's disease or ALS? How can we develop treatments or interventions for these diseases?
  • What are the genetic factors that contribute to brain development and function, and how do they interact with environmental influences?
  • What happens at the cellular level during brain injuries like concussions or strokes, and how can we promote neuroregeneration and recovery?

Questions Cognitive Science asks

  • How does the brain process and interpret sensory information to create perceptions of the world?
  • How do humans make decisions, and what cognitive processes are involved in these actions?
  • What mechanisms underlie the human ability to learn new languages? How do we store and retrieve words and grammar rules?
